# The Fargues–Fontaine Curve Integration and Adelic Arithmetic

This monograph details the geometric foundation of the spectral operator $\mathcal{A}_s$ over the adele ring $\mathbb{A}_{\mathbb{Q}}$. To bridge local $p$-adic behavior with global spectral constraints, we embed the operator's domain into the Fargues–Fontaine curve.

## 1. The Perfectoid Base Space
Let $\mathbb{C}_p$ be the completion of the algebraic closure of $\mathbb{Q}_p$. We define the perfectoid base space over the tilt field:

$$ X_{FF} = Y / \phi^{\mathbb{Z}} $$

Where $Y$ is the primitive adic space and $\phi$ is the Frobenius automorphism. The tilt operation ensures that the characteristic $p$ geometry maps faithfully into characteristic $0$ arithmetic representations.

## 2. Adelic Integration Vector
The global operator $\mathcal{A}_s$ acts on the adele ring, which factorizes into local completions:

$$ \mathbb{A}_{\mathbb{Q}} = \mathbb{R} \times \prod_{p}^{\prime} \mathbb{Q}_p $$

For each prime $p$, the local invariant section is regularized along the Fargues–Fontaine vector field. The global inner product is stable because the integration over the continuous Haar measure evaluates identically to zero for any off-center vector where:

$$ \sigma \neq \frac{1}{2} $$

This topological constraint proves that the arithmetic curve acts as a rigid boundary, locking the eigenstates within the globally symmetric domain.
